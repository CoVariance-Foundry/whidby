A: Flat Pairs Table

One row per (city, service) pair. All columns visible. Simple, sortable by any column. A city appears multiple times if it has multiple scored services.

Pros

  • Simple, familiar pattern
  • Sort by any column instantly
  • Every data point visible

Cons

  • City info repeats across rows
  • Hard to compare services within a city

B: City Rows + Service Chips

One row per city. Scored services appear as color-coded chips (green=high, yellow=mid, red=low) inside the row. City demographics never repeat. Click a chip for detail.

Pros

  • No repeated city info
  • See all services at a glance per city
  • Compact

Cons

  • Can't sort by service score directly
  • Chips get crowded with many services

C: Expandable City Rows

City rows show demographics + summary (best score, service count). Click to expand and see per-service detail rows with score, archetype, difficulty. Sort by best score at city level.

Pros

  • Clean separation of city vs. service data
  • Full detail without crowding
  • Sortable at city level

Cons

  • Requires clicking to see services
  • Harder to compare same service across cities
